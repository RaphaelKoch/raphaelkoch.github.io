Yoshihisa Maitani - Engineering the Future
August 20, 2021

In 1954 the world looked very different:

The European Union's Supreme Court rendered it's first verdict, NACA moved to Edwards Air Force Base (today's NASA Dryden Research Center) and a young Margaret Hamilton graduates from High School.

15 years later she would be hooked up to Houston's Mission Control from MIT in Massachussetts as Apollo 11's Lunar Module approached it'S last meter to the moon's surface. Aldrin, navigating only with her teams software in the Apollo Guidance Computer and his two eyes, radioed "Program Alarm" only 400 meters earlier.

As we now all know, the software would hold it's own.

Also Elvis started his career, Burger King opened it's first ever branch in Florida and in asia the french occupation of Vietnam ended only to spark more conflict for it's people over the years.

In Japan a company called Olympus employed a 21 year old automotive engineering student and aspiring photographer, Yoshihisa Maitani, who would patent his first camera design before he even left the Waseda University in Tokyo.



...Oh, and Godzilla released in Japan.
